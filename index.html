  1. Week 1: 14th to 20th January


    Siva Sir agrees to be our PI and gives our seniors an oppurtunity to recruit the juniors.

    The seniors present the idea of iGEM to the juniors and recruit the interested ones.

    We disperse for the semester exams after assembling the team.


    Week 2: 27th to end of January


    Our ideation phase starts. We decide to work on bioremediation to remove heavy metals from water

    We work on the initial components of our project.

  2. Week 3: 2nd to 9th February


    We have to modify our idea as we found a similar project already completed in the iGEM competition.

    We take steps to modify our idea. We consult with Dr. Alka Mehta to provide us some insights.


    Week 4: 12th to 19th February


    We refine our ocean acidification control idea for the approaching iBEC deadline.


    Week 5: 21st to 28th February


    We submit the completed manuscript to the iBEC website and await results. We disperse for the mid semester exams

  3. Week 6: 12th to 19th March


    IBEC results arrive!

    We are one of the five teams that get funding from the Department of Biotechnology to participate in iGEM.


    Week 7: 24th to 31st March


    We register for iGEM.


  4. Week 8: 23rd to 30th April


    We have our first Skype call with iGEM REC Chennai and talk about collaboration.

    Our kits arrived and we verified the contents.

  5. Weeks 9 & 10: 1st to 15th May


    We spend our summer leave time optimising protocols for culturing miniprep, digestion and ligation.


    Week 11: 21st to 27th May


    A member of our team visits NYU Abu Dhabi and we collaborate with them.

    We also have a Skype conference on the wiki page with them.

  6. Week 12: 1st to 7th June


    We begin our Human Practices work.

    We also obtain further feedback on the project by presenting our idea to Dr. Debashish Mishra.


    Week 13 & 14: 8th to 21st June


    We present our idea at Maharishi International Residential School, Sriperumbudur.

    We also perform a beach clean-up along with iGEM-REC and iGEM-IIT Madras teams.


    Week 15: 21st to 28th June


    We interview local fisherman relating to the recent changes in the catch rate.

  7. Week 16: 2nd to 8th July


    We start working for the interlab study. We contact Dr. Joseph Selvin to work in his lab for the interlab study.


    Week 17 & 18: 16th to 29th July


    We reach Dr. Selvin’s lab at the Pondicherry University and start our interlab work.

    We successfully complete the calibration part of the interlab.

    There seems to be some problem with the E.coli culture we use for the interlab study.

    We find contamination in our samples and we repeat the protocol.

    There is a persistent contamination problem so we had to leave Dr. Selvin’s lab and return to VIT.

  8. Week 19 & 20: 1st to 15th August


    We work for the interlab study by culturing a pure culture of E.coli.


    Week 21 & 22: 15th to 31st August


    We complete our interlab study and have our data accepted.

    We also apply for our visas.

    We order the necessary sequences from IDT.

    We order the restriction endonucleases and other enzymes we need to complete the 3A Assembly.

  9. Week 23: 1st to 7th September


    We present our ideas to our juniors and encourage them to participate in iGEM the next year. We start with the mathematical modelling of our system.


    Week 24 & 25: 8th to 21st September


    We attend the iGEM 2018 Virtual Conference conducted by NYU Abu Dhabi where we present our idea.

    We attend our Visa interviews.

  10. Week 26: 1st to 7th October


    We present our idea to the Vice President of VIT, Mr. Selvam

    We also synthesize our biobricks by digesting the respective part and ligating in the plasmid.


    Week 24: 8th to 14th October


    We send the ligated biobricks to iGEM.

    We complete all the residual work that remained before the big freeze.
